You're three booths into a Pokémon card show, holding a near-mint Charizard, and the dealer wants to know what you'd take for it. You pull out your phone. The pricing app shows a single number, the "market average," that's been sitting unchanged for two weeks. You both stare at it. Neither of you trusts it.

That's the problem TCGIQ exists to fix.

[**TCGIQ**](https://www.tcgiq.app/) is now live. It's a real-time pricing tool for trading cards, built for the moments where the price actually matters: at the booth, mid-negotiation, when the next move costs you money.

## Why Market Averages Aren't Enough

Most pricing tools quote a single number. It's an opaque average that smooths over condition, recency, and outliers. Those are the three things that actually move price.

TCGIQ shows **Fair Range** instead: the real low, median, and high from recent verified sales. Pull a card up, pick the condition (NM, LP, MP, HP, DMG), and you get a band that reflects how the market is actually trading right now, not where it traded three weeks ago.

And we show our work. Every Fair Range comes with the **five most recent sold listings** that backed the calculation, with date and price. If the range looks off, check the math yourself.

## Who It's For

Dealers working a booth. Collectors hunting deals. Traders mid-negotiation. Anyone who needs a fast, honest answer to "what's this actually worth?" without scrolling through stale comps or generic averages.

The core experience is free. A Pro tier adds graded card data (PSA 9/10, BGS, CGC) and Bundle Mode for negotiating multiple cards at once.
